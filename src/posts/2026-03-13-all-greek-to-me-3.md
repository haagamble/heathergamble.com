Today we visited **Acrocorinth**, the massive fortress rising above the ancient city of Corinth. Athens has the **Acropolis**. That got me thinking about prefixes. What does _acro_ mean? <!-- excerpt -->

While thinking about the word _acropolis_, I realized that the second part of the word comes from the Greek word **πόλη**, which means _city_. The word _acropolis_ literally means **“high city.”**

Moments like this make vocabulary easier to remember because the word connects to something we already know.

### Examples of Greek Words That Appear in English

| Greek   | Meaning       | English word that helps |
| ------- | ------------- | ----------------------- |
| πόλη    | city          | acropolis, metropolis   |
| μικρός  | small         | micro, microscope       |
| μεγάλος | big / large   | mega, megaphone         |
| ζωή     | life          | biology                 |
| ζώο     | animal        | zoo                     |
| βιβλίο  | book          | Bible, bibliography     |
| φως     | light         | photo, photograph       |
| φωνή    | voice / sound | telephone, phonics      |
| γραφή   | writing       | biography, photograph   |
| όνομα   | name          | anonymous, synonym      |
| χρόνος  | time          | chronology              |
| τόπος   | place         | topology                |
| κόσμος  | world         | cosmos                  |
| θερμός  | warm / heat   | thermometer             |
| καρδιά  | heart         | cardiac                 |
| γη      | earth / land  | geography               |
| παλαιός | ancient / old | paleolithic             |

Happy language learning!
